How to Read a Caster Size Chart

3‑minute guide · Updated 2025-08-01

For small distributors, the fastest way to avoid returns is to get plate size, bolt‑hole spacing, wheel diameter, and tread width right the first time.

The four numbers that matter

  1. Wheel diameter (e.g., 100/125/150mm): affects obstacle climbing and load rating.
  2. Tread width: wider spreads load and is often quieter.
  3. Top plate size: must match the mounting surface.
  4. Bolt‑hole spacing: center‑to‑center dimensions for screws/bolts.
Tip: Ask for a photo with a ruler on the old caster—this solves 80% of mismatches.
